Visual Hierarchy
Airbnb
airbnb.com

Visual Hierarchy tells you where your eyes should go on a page. The beauty of this principle is this is if it is done correctly, you won't have to think about where to look. Airbnb does this great by drawing your eyes to a beautiful picture of their product: vacation rentals. Your attention is hooked on the picture and gets you intrigued to learn more.

Rule of Thirds
Apple
apple.com

The rule of thirds states that a picture's focus should not be the focal point of the image itself. It should hit the "hot point" that are the corners of the center cell of a grid if it were to be placed over an image. This Apple mobile view shows this perfectly by having the two phone options not in the center, but they hit the hot corners of the center cell.

Hick's Law
Tesla
tesla.com

Hick's law, in relation to UI design, states that the user should never ask themselves: "What now?". Everything on a webpage should point the user to the next step. In the case of Tesla, the images change,but the "customer order" or "view inventory" buttons stay visible and obvious to make sure the user knows what to do next.
